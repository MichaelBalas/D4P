D4P¶

Kinesia Score: The number of keystrokes in 20 seconds¶

Kinesia Score is analyzed by comparing the mean bidirectional response times (in milliseconds) using the Wilcoxon signed-rank test.

The number of times both keys are hit are inversely proportional to the mean bidirectional response time. $$\textrm{keys hit} = k \cdot \mu_{bidirectional}^{-1} \textrm{ where } k=20\textrm{s} \cdot 1000 \textrm{ms/s}$$ Example: In the Dominant Hand with Horizontal Keyboard (Before) analysis, Subject_DL hit 29 keys:

$$\frac{20000 \textrm{ms}}{696.25 \textrm{ms}} \approx 29 \textrm{ total hit keys}$$

The data is analyzed using non-parametric tests due to the small sample size of 4. Normality tests (e.g. Kolmogorov-Smirnov or Shapiro-Wilk) are not applied because small samples most often pass (p > 0.05).

Dominant Hand with Horizontal Keyboard¶

### -------------------------
### Wilcoxon signed-rank test
### -------------------------

Input = ("
 Subject        Before               After
 subject_DL     696.25               650.9333333333333
 subject_JF     712.5714285714286    724.6666666666666
 subject_LV     369.51851851851853   362.0181818181818
 subject_PEV    547.5833333333334    605.96875
")

Data = read.table(textConnection(Input),header=TRUE)

wilcox.test(Data$Before, 
            Data$After, 
            paired=TRUE)

	Wilcoxon signed rank test

data:  Data$Before and Data$After
V = 4, p-value = 0.875
alternative hypothesis: true location shift is not equal to 0

Since the p-value is greater than 0.05, we conclude that the means have remained essentially unchanged (we accept the null hypothesis H0).

In [2]:
plot(Data$Before, Data$After, 
    pch = 4,
    xlab="Before",
    ylab="After")

abline(0,1, col="blue", lwd=2)

Plot of paired samples from a Wilcoxon signed-rank test. Data points above and to the left of the blue one-to-one line indicate observations with a higher value for After than for Before. The line of no improvement. Anything below the line is a decreased response time (i.e. improvement), and anything above is increased response time.

Conclusion¶

Unsurprisingly, none of the results for Kinesia score are statistically significant due to the very small sample size (n=4) and short time between tests (Before-After time = 1 month).
